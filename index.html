How To Use

Review the chapter in three deliberate moves.

Work from chronology to theme, then theme to argument. That keeps recall usable under timed pressure.

1

Start with the live chapter.

Lock in the range, turning points, and central theme before you try to write or quiz on it.

2

Link events to themes.

Connect politics, economics, society, and foreign policy so facts become patterns instead of isolated names.

3

Practice the argument.

Turn those patterns into thesis statements, contextualization, and evidence chains for AP-style writing.

Master the chronology, then use it to explain change, continuity, causation, and comparison.
